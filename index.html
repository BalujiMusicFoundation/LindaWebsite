A Life Lived
in Many Worlds

Linda Shanson — also known as Linda Shanovitch — is a British singer, songwriter, dancer, writer, poet and anthropologist whose extraordinary life spans continents, cultures and art forms. With a BSc in Anthropology, she has lived and worked in Mexico, India and Paris, drawing on each experience to create music that is vivid, searching and deeply human.

Her first published song, Winter in Spring, was broadcast on the BBC when she was just 13 years old, published through the legendary Northern Songs. That early spark never dimmed. Decades later, she is celebrated as a vocalist who weaves jazz, world music, Indian classical and original compositions into something entirely her own.

As the wife and long-time artistic partner of the acclaimed blind sitarist and composer Baluji Shrivastav OBE — who has collaborated with Stevie Wonder, Annie Lennox and Shakira — Linda has played a central role in one of the most remarkable musical partnerships in contemporary world music.

Orchestra

Inner Vision Orchestra

The world's only professional ensemble of blind and visually impaired musicians — a groundbreaking achievement in music and inclusion.

Breaking Barriers Through Music

Linda Shanson co-founded the Inner Vision Orchestra in 2012 alongside her husband Baluji Shrivastav OBE. Born from a desire to overcome the discrimination that blind musicians face, the orchestra has grown into a celebrated eighteen-piece ensemble drawing musicians from around the world.

Their repertoire moves across songs from Iran, Lebanon, Afghanistan, India and Nigeria, through soulful Gospel and Blues, to sublime Indian Ragas and Western Classical compositions. BBC Radio 3's Sean Rafferty called them a "magical mixture of sounds."

Linda serves as Manager of the Orchestra, driving its vision, performances and outreach programmes that transform lives through music education and performance.

Foundation

Baluji Music
Foundation

In 2008, Linda Shanson and Baluji Shrivastav OBE co-founded the Baluji Music Foundation, a registered UK charity dedicated to transforming the lives of blind and visually impaired musicians.

The Foundation promotes participation of disabled people in music at the highest professional level — from education and mentorship to world-class performance. It founded the Inner Vision Orchestra and runs creative projects in schools, hospitals and care settings across Greater London.

Through partnerships with organisations like Live Music Now, the Foundation continues to expand its reach, creating opportunities for blind musicians that simply did not exist before.

IBSA World Games Anthem

Linda Shanson composed the official IBSA World Games Anthem, performed at the Opening Ceremony of the 2023 IBSA World Games at Symphony Hall, Birmingham — one of the UK's greatest concert halls — by the Inner Vision Orchestra. A landmark moment for blind sport and blind music, united on one stage.

Poetry & Lyrics

Throughout her career Linda has been a poet as well as a lyricist — her words flowing between sung verse and written page. She served as poet for Raaja-Ajaar, a commissioned work where she narrated and sang her own texts. Her lyrics span English, Hindi and beyond, rooted in her life across three continents.
