Bio

A Life Lived in Many Worlds

Linda Shanson is an artist, producer and founder of the Baluji Music Foundation and manager of the Inner Vision Orchestra. With a BSc Hons in Anthropology, she has researched and worked in Mexico, India and Paris.

Her first published song, Winter in Spring, was broadcast on the BBC when she was just 13 years old, published through the legendary Northern Songs. As a singer and songwriter she has several albums with Jazz Orient / Re-Orient on ARC International and has performed extensively across the UK, Europe and Oman.

She composes and sings in various musical styles including Indian vocals. Her extra-vocal improvisations earned her the title of "Bjork on Helium" by the London Evening Standard. She is a published author, winning first prize in the Elmbridge Literary Competition.

She has also worked extensively with her husband Baluji Shrivastav OBE — the blind sitar maestro who has collaborated with Stevie Wonder, Annie Lennox and Shakira — as a performer and producer of many innovative projects.

Music

Once Upon a Night

Jazz Orient (aka Re-Orient, when they were signed to the ARC Music label) had not played together for 10 years, due to being busy with other projects, plus 2 years of the pandemic.

Then one evening, by chance, they found they were all in Chris's home city of Leicester — with instruments! So they went out for dinner at a chaat house on Leicester's Golden Mile, and then on to his Aloft Studio. There they improvised 5 tracks — just like that. No plan or instructions. They just played.

The magic was still there! Their 33 year, 7 album (and 1 single) association gave each of them an instinct or shared memory, for sensing what the other 2 might do at certain times. The tracks are in the order in which they were played.
